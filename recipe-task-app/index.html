Recipes are written to read, not always to cook from

SaySo helps turn the recipe into the working checklist you need in the kitchen.

A recipe page can contain the right information and still be frustrating to use while cooking. The ingredients may be separated from the instructions. The page may include long introductions, ads, notes, substitutions, comments, and repeated scrolling. Once your hands are wet, floury, or busy at the stove, even a simple tap can feel like an interruption.

SaySo is a recipe task app for iPhone that helps turn recipe pages into guided cooking steps. Instead of treating the recipe like an article you have to keep rereading, SaySo helps organize the actions into a task flow. That means you can focus on what happens next: prep the vegetables, heat the pan, add the garlic, simmer, taste, plate, and clean up.

Why task steps help in the kitchen

Cooking is a sequence of small decisions. You need to know what to do now, what is coming next, and whether anything needs to happen in parallel. A good recipe task list makes those decisions easier because the current step is separated from the surrounding text. It also helps when you get interrupted, because the next action is easier to find than a paragraph halfway down a recipe page.

SaySo is especially useful for recipes you are trying for the first time, meal prep sessions with several moving parts, or cooking from a video or article while your phone sits across the counter. The app is not trying to replace the original recipe. It is trying to make the recipe easier to execute when you are actively cooking.

Private and practical

SaySo works without an account, stores tasks locally by default, and does not use tracking analytics or sell your data. Optional sign-in is available for cloud sync. That setup is intentionally practical: you should be able to turn a recipe into steps quickly, use it for dinner, and move on without creating a whole productivity system first.
